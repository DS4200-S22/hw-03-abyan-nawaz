The data type shown are attributes in which categorical data is shown for each category that is being compared. The data type is also quantitative as the amount of each smaller category, which is differentiated by different colors within each bar, within each category (bar) is measured in amount by the y-axis. The marks within this image are lines that make up the bars. The channels used are the horizontal and vertical positioning of the bars, the different colors used within each bar, and the area of the lines being expanded into the shapes of a bars. The marks used are links, specifically containment marks as the areas of the bars are used to compare different subcategories between major categories as well as between major categories. In other words, the larger categories per bar are compared with one another, and the subcategories are being compared with one another within the categories (which is represented by different colors in each bar.

Items exist within this visualization as nodes of a network. These nodes are then connected by links which are used to show relationships between the different nodes within a mind map, such as “Ideas,” “Categories,” and “Themes.” Points are used for different “Ideas,” “Category,” and “Theme” and lines are used to connect between each of point to represent a relationship. Different colors are used for “Ideas,” “Category,” and “Theme,” the areas of each point are different, and the positioning of the points are placed so that the “Ideas” branch out from either the “Theme” or “Category.”

Items are used within this visualization as nodes. Also, there are lines between each node to signify a relationship between the two nodes. The points are different colors to signify different nodes. These points are horizontally and vertically positioned throughout the visualization to show the change in rankings.
